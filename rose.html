Roseanne Park, known professionally as Rosé, is a South Korean-New Zealand singer and dancer. She was born on February 11, 1997, in Auckland, New Zealand. Rosé rose to fame as a member of the internationally acclaimed K-pop girl group Blackpink, where she showcases her talents as the main vocalist and lead dancer. Rosé moved to Melbourne, Australia, during her childhood and attended Canterbury Girls' Secondary College. She was discovered by YG Entertainment through their auditions in Sydney, Australia, in 2012. After joining the company, Rosé trained for several years before debuting with Blackpink in 2016. Throughout her career, Rosé has garnered praise for her powerful vocals, emotional performances, and captivating stage presence. She has contributed to Blackpink's chart-topping hits such as "Playing with Fire," "Kill This Love," "Lovesick Girls," and more. Rosé's unique voice and style have made her a beloved figure in the global music industry, earning her recognition as one of the most influential artists in K-pop.
